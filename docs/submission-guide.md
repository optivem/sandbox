# Submission Guide

## How to Submit Your Work

1. Go to the [**Reviews Dashboard**](https://optivem.github.io/reviews/)
2. Find the row for your module and click **+** in your project's column
3. This opens a pre-filled issue — click **Create** to submit it (do not change the title or body)
4. When you're ready for review, add a comment on the issue with:
   - A link to your repository
   - For each item in the Review Checklist, confirm how you satisfied it

## The Learning Process

For every Practical Module in this program:

1. You attempt to implement the Practical Module in your project
2. You submit the Practical Module (see [How to Submit Your Work](#how-to-submit-your-work) above)
3. We'll mark it as **In Review** in our Dashboard
4. We'll review it and provide you with feedback based on our Review Checklist, we'll mark it as **In Progress** which means you have some work to do
5. You make updates based on our feedback, and comment again, so that we review again
6. We repeat the process until you've passed all the items from our Checklist
7. At the end, we'll mark your submission as **Done** in the Dashboard
8. If you face any additional challenges, try to reproduce those challenges and reply on the same community thread so that we can support you

We go through the same process, module-by-module. This means that during our program, you are learning and applying it on your project - we're working incrementally & iteratively.

> **Important:** If the steps inside the module aren't clear or don't work or you get stuck, create a ticket and write the questions and challenges that are blocking you.

## Review Process

All statuses are updated automatically — you don't need to change them manually.

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Open** | Ticket created, waiting for you to submit for review |
| **In Review** | You've commented, waiting for reviewer |
| **In Progress** | Reviewer is working on feedback |
| **Done** | Review complete, ticket closed |

When you've addressed feedback, add a comment on your issue describing what you changed. The status will automatically move back to **In Review**.

Each item will be marked with one of the following: PASS, NEEDS WORK.

At the end, when everyone completed, I'll write **SANDBOX DONE**.

## If You Face Challenges

If you face challenges that prevent you from completing a module, you can ask for support:

- **Alignment challenges:** If you face challenges getting alignment / buy-in or aren't sure how to communicate, you can tell me which arguments / push-backs you've been receiving, so that I can provide advice.
- **Technical challenges:** Perhaps you're facing implementation challenges. You're not sure how to apply it, maybe you see it as too hard, or take too long... If possible for you, you can reproduce it in the Sandbox too.

> **Important:** If the steps inside the module aren't clear or don't work or you get stuck, create a ticket and write the questions and challenges that are blocking you.

## Real Project Reflection

***Pilots go to Flight Simulator Training before they fly a real airplane.***

As you complete each module, we encourage you to try applying what you've learned to your Real Life Project. The goal is to identify gaps early — were you prepared, or did you face surprises or challenges?

At the end of each module, there is an optional **Real Life Reflection**. This is where you report back on your experience applying the module's concepts to your real project:

- **Were you prepared?** Did the sandbox exercises give you enough to apply this confidently?
- **Did you face surprises or challenges?** Anything that went wrong or was unexpected?
- **Were there gaps?** Anything the module didn't cover that you needed in practice?

To submit a reflection:

1. Open a comment on your existing review issue (or create a new one)
2. Comment on whether you were prepared for adopting this in your real project, or if you faced surprises or challenges

### If You Face Challenges in Your Real Life Project

1. **Try to reproduce the challenge in your Sandbox Project.** The sandbox uses a different domain, so you can share it freely without any confidentiality concerns. If you can reproduce the problem there, post it in the group chat — I can look at your sandbox code directly and help you resolve it.
2. **If you can't reproduce it in the sandbox**, describe the challenge in general terms (without sharing proprietary code or details) and ask in the group chat. I'll do my best to advise based on the description.

> **Important:** Do not share proprietary source code, internal architecture details, or any information covered by your company's NDA in the group chat. The sandbox exists precisely so you can get hands-on help without risking confidentiality.

### Scope

1. I'll be reviewing your Sandbox Project and tracking progress of completing milestones
2. I will NOT be reviewing your Real Life Project, however I will be tracking progress based on your self-reported progress updates

### Real Life Project Review (Consulting)

If you're an Engineering Leader and want to request me to also review your Real Life Project, then that requires a separate consulting engagement and that I sign an NDA with your company. That would enable you to share your Real Life Project source/test/pipeline and receive my review/feedback, as well as access to a private chat group with your company's pilot team. If you're thinking about this, you can send me a DM.

## Note for Teams

If you're registered as part of a team, then there should be only one person who posts this, on behalf of the whole team. This means you need to designate someone as the Project Lead who will be responsible for submitting your project for review.
